Eutrophication

Eutrophication is the process by which bodies of water become enriched with minerals and nutrients. While eutrophication occurs naturally, it is also greatly accelerated by human intervention. Agriculture is a major source of runoff into water habitats. Chemical fertilizers and animal manure contribute nitrogen and phosphorous into both fresh water and coastal waterways. These nutrients promote plant and algal growth, choking aquatic life, and contaminating water sources for both human and animal consumption. Eutrophication can lead to hypoxia, also known as dead zones, causing fish kills and a decrease in aquatic life, as animals entering these areas may be unable to escape before suffocation occurs.
